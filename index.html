WHY I CHOSE PROGRAMMING

I've always thought that building a program — whether it’s a simple website or a more
complex application — is something cool. There’s something magical about typing lines
of code and watching them come alive on the screen. It's like you're creating something
from nothing, and that creation can help people, tell stories, or simply look beautiful.
Right now, I’m learning the basics: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. These are the foundations
of web development, and although they seem simple at first, they actually offer so much
depth and power. I enjoy exploring how layouts are made, how animations work, and how
interactive elements respond to user actions. It's like giving life to the ideas inside my head.

MY PASSION FOR DRAWING

Besides programming, I also have a deep love for drawing — especially drawing chibi characters. I find them easier to draw than full-body figures, and they always carry this cute, cheerful energy that makes me smile. Whether it's a little character waving at the viewer or holding a rice ball, drawing chibi gives me a break from the logic-heavy world of code and lets me express creativity in a light-hearted way. I often draw digitally using my handphone(i want tablet), and I hope to someday publish or exhibit my drawings online — maybe even in the form of webcomics or game characters I make myself.

MY LOVE FOR
JAPANESE CULTURE

My love for anime naturally led me to explore more about Japanese culture. I love everything about it — the food like sushi, ramen, mochi, and dango; the festivals with lanterns and yukata; the calm atmosphere of small towns and shrines; and of course, the beauty of miko outfits in traditional ceremonies.

There’s something about Japan’s balance of modern technology and deep tradition that feels very comforting and inspiring.

I truly hope that one day I can visit Japan, walk the streets of Kyoto, taste authentic ramen, and maybe even attend Comiket or Ghibli Museum.

NASI GORENG & SOLITUDE

Among all this, one thing that has been a quiet companion in my journey is… nasi goreng. There’s something special about eating nasi goreng in the middle of the night, especially when you're alone.

It's warm, a little spicy, and comforting — like a hug in food form.

Just like anime, nasi goreng has been with me when I stayed up late watching shows, drawing characters, or struggling with code. It became more than just food. It became comfort.
